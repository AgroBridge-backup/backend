### 3. Celebration & Recognition Rituals

1.  **Weekly Wins & Gratitude (Friday Afternoons):** A dedicated Slack channel (`#weekly-wins`) where every team member shares one professional win and gives a public "thank you" to a colleague who helped them.
2.  **The "Unsung Hero" Award (Monthly All-Hands):** A peer-nominated award given to a team member who made a significant impact behind the scenes. The winner receives a small bonus and company-wide recognition.
3.  **Failure Friday (Bi-weekly, Optional):** A safe-space meeting where team members can share a lesson from something that didn't work. The goal is to normalize failure as a key part of learning and innovation.
4.  **Milestone Bell:** When a major feature is deployed or a significant sales goal is hit, the person responsible rings a physical (or virtual) bell in the main Slack channel, followed by a shower of celebratory emojis from the team.
5.  **"Story Time" with a Producer (Quarterly):** We invite one of our producers to a company-wide call to share their story and how AgroBridge has impacted their work. This keeps us connected to our mission.

### 4. Weekly Founder's Inspiration (Templates)

*   **Subject: A Lesson from History**
    > Team, I was reading about the construction of the Roman aqueducts—how they built structures that have lasted 2,000 years with simple tools and an unwavering vision. That's us. We are building digital aqueducts of trust. Every stable API, every clean line of code, is a stone laid for a future we can't yet see. Be proud of the infrastructure you're building. It's meant to last.

*   **Subject: The Power of a Single Question**
    > This week, I challenge you to ask one question: "How can I make this 1% better?" Not 100%, just 1%. A slightly clearer variable name, a slightly kinder piece of feedback, a slightly more efficient query. The compounding effect of these small acts of excellence is what creates a great company.

### 5. Stoic & Historical Parables

*   **On Facing Technical Debt (The Parable of the Leaky Roof):**
    > A man complained he had no time to fix his leaky roof because he was too busy bailing water out of his house. It's easy to get caught up in bailing water. Sometimes, the most effective work is to stop, endure the short-term discomfort of the rain, and fix the roof. Let's not be afraid to stop and fix the roof.

*   **On Teamwork (The Parable of the Stonecutters):**
    > A traveler saw three stonecutters and asked them what they were doing. The first said, "I'm cutting a stone." The second said, "I'm earning a living." The third looked up with a gleam in his eye and said, "I'm building a cathedral." We are all building a cathedral. Never forget the bigger picture of what we are creating together.

### 3. Rituales de Celebración y Reconocimiento

1.  **Victorias y Gratitud Semanal (Viernes por la tarde):** Un canal de Slack (`#victorias-semanales`) donde cada miembro comparte un logro profesional y da un "gracias" público a un colega que le ayudó.
2.  **Premio "Héroe Anónimo" (Reunión Mensual):** Un premio nominado por pares para un miembro del equipo que tuvo un impacto significativo tras bastidores.
3.  **Viernes de Fracaso (Quincenal, Opcional):** Una reunión en un espacio seguro para compartir una lección de algo que no funcionó. El objetivo es normalizar el fracaso como parte del aprendizaje.
4.  **Campana de Hitos:** Cuando se despliega una gran característica o se alcanza un objetivo de ventas, el responsable toca una campana (física o virtual) en el canal principal de Slack.
5.  **"Tiempo de Historias" con un Productor (Trimestral):** Invitamos a uno de nuestros productores a una llamada para que comparta su historia y cómo AgroBridge ha impactado su trabajo.

### 5. Parábolas Estoicas e Históricas

*   **Sobre Enfrentar la Deuda Técnica (La Parábola del Techo con Goteras):**
    > Un hombre se quejaba de que no tenía tiempo para arreglar su techo con goteras porque estaba demasiado ocupado sacando agua de su casa. Es fácil quedar atrapado sacando agua. A veces, el trabajo más efectivo es detenerse, soportar la incomodidad a corto plazo de la lluvia y arreglar el techo. No tengamos miedo de detenernos y arreglar el techo.

*   **Sobre el Trabajo en Equipo (La Parábola de los Canteros):**
    > Un viajero vio a tres canteros y les preguntó qué estaban haciendo. El primero dijo: "Estoy cortando una piedra". El segundo dijo: "Me estoy ganando la vida". El tercero levantó la vista con un brillo en los ojos y dijo: "Estoy construyendo una catedral". Todos estamos construyendo una catedral. Nunca olviden la visión más grande de lo que estamos creando juntos.
